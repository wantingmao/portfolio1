Flower Power Brush

An adaptive hairbrush designed to be mainstream and visually appealing

Collaborators: Qiqi Zhou, Iris Chen, Alice Zhang





Problem Statement

Brushing one’s hair is an everyday action and most of the time, a necessity. However, most conventional hairbrushes are built in a way that requires finger dexterity and strength.

Current Hairbrushes...

  • require finger dexterity, fine motor skills, and gross mobility 
  • can be too large/bulky to transport out
  • can have an even smaller gripping handle if you opt for the smaller, portable options

Competitive Analysis

Scalp Massager Shampoo Brush

  • Pros: ergonomic, requires minimal finger dexterity and strength, minimal need for  wrist mobility
  • Cons: limited use

Tangle Teaser

  • Pros: compact due to lack of handle
  • Cons: requires finger dexterity and strength in order to grip the hairbrush 

Folding Travel Mirror Hair Brushes

  • Pros: compact
  • Cons: requires both hands to open, requires finger dexterity, grip and strength

Home adaptation

  • requires someone else to tie and remove a hair band or rope around the brush onto the user's wrist

Style Inspiration

Sandy Liang

  • known for its whimsical and playful charm accompanied by a sense of innocent nostalgia reminiscent of girlhood
  • characterized by the presence of flowers, lace, and ribbons, harmonizing with a soft color palette

- Why can’t assistive products also be aesthetically pleasing
and commercially mainstream? -

Render 1: Small Brush Prototype

Render 1 + Magnet clasp for handle

  • Pros: ease of adjustment, can be fitted using one hand, requires minimal fine motor skill and strength
  • Cons: bulky, not the most secure

Render 2: D Handle


  • Sourced the brush bristles from a travel compact brush
  • Used ratios to adjust from the small prototype to render 2
  • Ergonomic handle created in Adobe illustrator before being imported into Fusion360

3D printed D-shape handle

  • Pros: cohesive design, ergonomic finger placements, minimizes risk of catching loose strands, more stability for grasping, intuitive.
  • Cons: no adjustment, might not fit differing hand sizes

Render 2: User Testing

Was able to work on different hand sizes

Reflections + Future Iterations

  • Would like to try out different handle attachments/materials
  • How do different attachments affect user interaction?
  • Continuation of material exploration (different filaments, molds even)

  • We only tested on individuals who had full function of their fingers so in the future we would like to test the product on individuals who lack fine motor skills and finger dexterity, then adjust accordingly
  • Could even adapt this for other hygiene products (ex: shower scrubs)
