The process of my daily practice

  1. Take in the Prompt​
  • I began by compiling the Productober prompts from 2019-2022
  • Immediately after I complete my product ideation sketch for the day, I take in the next prompt
  • This gives me the entire night and following day to come up with an idea
  • I don't always follow the prompt, in the case where I have a different idea or if I'm having trouble with the prompt

  1. Ideation

In the beginning of my 100 Days challenge, I was fortunate to have ideas spontaneously pop into my head maybe 5/10 times. A few weeks passed and as one would expect, the frequency of that happening decreased more and more. These were some tactics I used to help me ideate:

  • Ask questions. Broaden the prompt or think of all the things that relate to the prompt
  • Ex. prompt: “coffee”. What’s the process of making coffee? What instruments are used to make coffee? What’s the process of consuming coffee? Why do people drink coffee?
  • Consider the current issues or negative experiences that I, or the people I know, have had with the product at hand. Think about how I can change/improve it.
  • A random object generator is my savior sometimes (for inspiration, brainstorming, adding randomness to my practice)
  • Perchance Object Generator
  • The Word Finder Object Generator
  • Thinking about other objects in combination with the prompt can give me some inspiration as well
  • A Google search: types of “ (insert prompt) ”
  • More often than not, I have minimal knowledge of the prompt given to me… (If I have no idea how a printer works, how am I supposed to design one??)
  • Turning to aesthetics.
  • If I really can’t think of an “original” and functional product idea, I will just focus on how it looks and how I can make something related to the prompt that looks pleasing to the eye
  • Asking others for suggestions
  • Other people usually have refreshing and innovative takes on the prompt or have a new prompt I can work with (repetitive prompts can be really hard to work with)
  • Scroll through other product design accounts on Instagram/other social media platforms
  • Pinterest and Google are my best friends when it comes to brainstorming

  1. Research

Before Ideation

  • Searching up: Types of “prompt” or “prompt” designs​
  • especially when I'm unfamiliar with the topic​
  • this gives me a sense of what already exists

After coming up with an idea, I consider...

  • Does the product I’ve come up with already exist?​
  • What have other people created that relate to this prompt?​
  • How can I differentiate my product?
  • What can I add/change to these existing products to improve them?

What I've learned and the mindset that has helped me through this process

One of my main struggles with forming habits used to be trying to make things perfect or over-exerting myself in the beginning. At the beginning of these 100 days, I found myself trying to come up with an idea 3 days in advance but once I became consciously aware of my previous bad habits while trying to build good habits, I was able to stop myself, realizing that it really just defeats the purpose of my practice.

I’ve learned that the point is to do something small each day that I can incorporate into an existing routine, and therefore, I’ll gradually improve without burning out. I’ve also realized that it’s also not about setting goals, which is what I’m used to hearing and doing, but it’s actually about the system. It’s the process and the act of making that will give me the results I want. Likewise, the product of my family practice doesn't have to be perfect either. By focusing on the system instead, I’ll be less likely to get discouraged and disappointed which has often led me to break my habits in the past.

Treating myself as a corporation, as an entity that is separate from me. I find that because I usually care so much about the quality and outcome of my projects, part of my resistance to accomplishing what I desire stems from fear of disappointment. If I step outside of myself, however, and think objectively about my work, not only is that pressure and fear lessened, but I’ll also have a far better chance at improvement. This mindset also helped me meet deadlines and accomplish my personal goals because it would give me a more structured schedule. I would be less likely to overwhelm myself and burn out while maintaining frequent mental and physical checkups.
